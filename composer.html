About the Composer

Gérard Pape is a composer of contemporary and electronic music. His work explores sound, psychology, and complex sonic structures, blending acoustic instruments with advanced electronic systems.

Biography

Born in Brooklyn (New York, 1955), Gérard Pape studied both music composition and clinical psychology at the University of Michigan.

He is also a trained Lacanian psychoanalyst, which strongly influences his approach to composition, particularly his interest in perception, memory, and “sound fantasies”.

In the early 1990s, he moved to France where he became director of the Ateliers UPIC (now CCMIX), an important center for computer-assisted composition.

His work has been influenced by composers such as Iannis Xenakis and Julio Estrada, focusing on chaos theory, timbre evolution, and sonic trajectories.

Highlights

• Director of CCMIX (UPIC studios) from 1991 to 2007

• Founder of CLSI (Cercle pour la Libération du Son et de l’Image)

• Over 50+ works including orchestral, chamber, vocal and electronic music

• Performed internationally in Europe, USA, Asia and South America
